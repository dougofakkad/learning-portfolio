Braking System

Vented front Disc

The front brake disc should be Inspected for thickness, warping and a clear contact face.

Brake Pads

Inspect the brake pad thickness, and ensure wear sensor is located correctly.

Caliper Carrier

Inspect the Carrier for correct fitment of the Pad locating springs.

Front Caliper

Inspect the Front Caliper for any signs of leaking from the dual piston seals and ensure that it has free movement on the Caliper sliders.

Solid Rear Disc

The rear brake disc should be inspected for thickness, warping and a clear contact face.

Rear Brake pads

Inspect the Brake pad thickness, and ensure wear sensor is located correctly.

Electronic Parking Brake Motor

Ensure Electronic Parking Brake Motor is free of any damage and debris and is secured correctly on the back of the Caliper.

Rear Caliper

Inspect the rear caliper for any signs of leaking from the single piston seal and ensure that it has free movement on the Caliper sliders.

Foot brake operation

Pressurised brake fluid is forced into the brake caliper, which then acts upon the piston moving it outwards onto the brake pad. This action causes the caliper to move towards the inner of the assembly and clamp the two brake pads on either side of the brake discs.

Parking Brake Opperation

An electrical signal is sent to the EPB motor and activates a gear system that forces a mechnical lever onto the back of the rear caliper piston assembly. This action allows the same clamping motion to the brake Discs and pads, but also allows the mechanisim to be locked in place untill a release command is sent via the driver EPB button.

If working on Rear Brakes, ensure the electronic parking brake is deselected before any work is carried out.

Disconect the brake pad warning wire.

For the rear caliper only, remove electronic parking brake motor and back off caliper internals (turn spindle anti-clockwise).

Remove brake caliper by loosening & removing the bottom caliper bolt to allow the caliper to be pivioted upwards, then slide the unit off the top slider.

Remove both brake pads and locating springs.

Remove caliper carrier.

Remove disc locating grub screw and lift off brake disc.

Installation can be in reverse order. Technicians must ensure the hub is clean for the brake disc to locate to and brake grease must be applied to the brake pad locating springs and the caliper sliders.
